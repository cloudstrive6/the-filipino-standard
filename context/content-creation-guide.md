# The Filipino Standard - Content Creation Guide

> The master guide for writing scroll-stopping posts. Anyone (human or AI) writing for TFS reads this before drafting.

---

## 1. The Universal Post Structure

Every TFS post, regardless of platform, follows the same 5-part skeleton. The lengths change. The skeleton doesn't.

### 1. HOOK (first 1-2 lines)

**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. Earn the next line.

The hook is the most expensive real estate on the page. If a reader doesn't continue past line 2, nothing else matters. The hook must do exactly one of three things: provoke **curiosity**, deliver **shock**, or trigger **recognition** ("oh, I've felt this too").

### 2. CONTEXT (2-3 short paragraphs)

**Purpose:** Set up the problem, the comparison, or the situation. Frame the stakes.

Don't lecture. Don't summarize Wikipedia. Drop the reader into a specific scene: a Meralco bill, a queue at BIR, an Auckland household opening an envelope. Specificity is credibility.

### 3. EVIDENCE (2-3 paragraphs)

**Purpose:** Earn the argument with facts, data, examples, and constitutional citations.

This is where the research brief earns its keep. Numbers, sources, the Constitution verbatim. The reader should feel the floor is solid under them, not that you're vibing.

### 4. PIVOT / INSIGHT (1-2 paragraphs)

**Purpose:** The "aha" moment. The reframe. The deeper truth that the news cycle isn't telling.

This is where TFS earns its name. Anyone can cite numbers. We're here to tell people *what the numbers mean.* "This isn't a one-off, it's a pattern." "This isn't bad luck, it's a design choice."

### 5. CLOSER (1-2 lines)

**Purpose:** Land. Hard.

A verdict line. A rhetorical question. A callback to the hook. Never a soft "what do you think?" wind-down. The closer is the line readers screenshot and share.

## 2. Hook Formulas (with our own examples)

These are battle-tested. Pull from this list, vary across posts, never default to the same formula twice in a row.

### Contrast hook
> *"In NZ, the power company pays YOU every year. In the Philippines, Meralco just takes."*

Pattern: [Better reality] + [Worse reality]. The grammar should be parallel.

### Question hook
> *"When did Filipinos stop getting angry?"*

Pattern: A question the reader cannot scroll past without trying to answer.

### Statement hook
> *"You know a country is really messed up when..."*

Pattern: Open with a setup the reader recognizes as universal, then specify.

### Number hook
> *"Every month, over 8 million Filipino households pay Meralco. None of them have ever received a single peso back."*

Pattern: A number large enough to anchor, paired with a sharper observation.

### Personal hook
> *"I've been living in NZ for over 10 years, and one thing that still amazes me..."*

Pattern: First-person credibility, then a specific observation. Used sparingly so it doesn't become a tic.

### Reframe hook
> *"That's not resilience. That's surrender."*

Pattern: Take a word the public uses with pride and gently flip its meaning.

### Repetition hook
> *"The roads? Still broken. The electricity bills? Still insane. The schools? Still underfunded."*

Pattern: Three parallel beats, same syntax, escalating weight.

### Single-sentence hook variants

- **The confession:** *"I love my country. That's why I refuse to lie about it."*
- **The accusation:** *"You're not paying for electricity. You're paying for a private profit margin."*
- **The dare:** *"Name a Meralco rate cut that lasted longer than the news cycle."*

## 3. Narrative Techniques

These are the tools we use throughout the body, not only at the hook.

### Parallel structure for emphasis
> *"The people pay. The oligarchs profit. The Constitution watches."*

Three beats. Same grammar. The third lands the verdict.

### Repetition for rhythm
> *"Every. Single. Time."*

Periods between words. Forces the reader to slow down. Useful when the post has been building cognitive load and you need to drop them back into the line.

### Contrast pairs
> *"One government is solving problems. The other one IS the problem."*

The most powerful sentence in the TFS toolkit. Use sparingly so it stays sharp.

### The "imagine if" technique
> *"Imagine if every Filipino household received PHP 13,000 from Meralco every year. Now read your last electric bill."*

Paint the alternative reality concretely. Then drop them back into the actual one.

### The callback
> *"Remember the post we did on Entrust? Now do the math on how many Meralco bills that PHP 13,000 would have covered."*

Reference an earlier post to build a connected narrative. Readers feel the page is going somewhere, not just posting in circles.

### The "here's what nobody talks about" reveal
> *"Here's what nobody mentions: Meralco has never been community-owned. Not in 1903. Not in 1962. Not now."*

Promise the reader they're about to know something most people don't. Then deliver.

### The rhetorical question closer
> *"Kailan pa ba?"*
> *"When do we stop watching?"*
> *"How many more SONAs?"*

The question is the verdict. Don't soften it with "let me know in the comments."

### The verdict line
> *"Ganun kalala."*

Short. Final. Devastating. Often Tagalog. Often a single phrase. This is the line readers screenshot. Earn it with the 200 words above it.

## 4. Taglish Placement (the rule, restated)

From `brand-context.md`, with examples:

### Use Taglish for emotional peaks. Never for information delivery.

| Where it belongs | Where it doesn't |
|---|---|
| Closer / verdict line | Inside a factual sentence |
| Rhetorical question at the end | The hook of a Reddit post for non-Filipino readers |
| A standalone exclamation between paragraphs | As filler "for flavor" |
| A line of recognition the reader silently nods at | A translation of an English thought |

### Examples in context

**GOOD - Taglish carries the closer:**
> "...And after all of that - the bills, the queues, the broken promises - the news cycle moves on and we go back to scrolling.
>
> Ganun kalala."

**GOOD - Taglish punctuates between English paragraphs:**
> "Meralco's ownership has changed hands six times since 1903. Not once has it landed with the people who actually pay the bills.
>
> Sino ba talaga ang nagbabayad?
>
> Eight million households. None of them on the cap table."

**BAD - Sprinkled, translated-sounding:**
> "The kuryente is so mahal kasi the company doesn't really care about the consumers..."

**BAD - Used to deliver information:**
> "Meralco serves over 8 million households na nagbabayad every month..."

### Test before publishing

Read the post aloud. If a Tagalog phrase feels like a code-switch a real person would make at that exact emotional moment, keep it. If it sounds like a translator looking for "local flavor," delete it and rewrite in English.

### Tagalog grammar verification (CRITICAL)

AI models frequently make Tagalog grammar errors. Wrong pronoun forms ("tayo" vs "natin" vs "namin"), unnatural word order, and incorrect verb focus are the three most common failure modes. Before finalizing any Tagalog text in a post, verify the grammar is correct against `brand-context.md` Section 4 (Tagalog Grammar Rules).

When in doubt, keep Tagalog to short, punchy phrases (1-2 sentences maximum) rather than risk grammatical errors in longer passages. The emotional impact of a Taglish switch comes from the *moment*, not the length.

**Pre-cleared safe closers** (use confidently):

- "Ganun kalala."
- "Kailan pa ba?"
- "Hindi tayo dapat sanay sa mediocre."
- "Dapat idemand natin ang gobyernong gumagana."

If a draft uses any Tagalog phrase that is NOT on the safe list and NOT a clean equivalent of one of the GOOD examples in `brand-context.md`, the draft is held for grammar verification before publish.

## 5. Image Prompt Strategy

Every post gets a unique, post-specific image direction. **There is no fixed template.** The image is part of the message.

### 5.1 Match image style to post tone

| Post tone | Image style |
|---|---|
| Political commentary, satire, biting | Satirical editorial cartoon, single-panel |
| Data, comparison, numbers | Clean infographic or split-screen comparison |
| Empowerment, hope, identity | Editorial illustration, painterly, warm |
| Side-by-side governance comparison | Comic strip / two-panel illustration |
| Constitutional / historical | Document-style, parchment-feel, with a single bold modern overlay |
| Reactive / breaking | Photojournalism-style, news-rooted |

### 5.2 The image should stand alone

Test: if someone screenshots only the image (no caption, no link), does it still convey the core idea? If not, the image is decoration, not communication. Decoration is acceptable but rarely the right call.

The strongest TFS images are the ones a reader will save and re-share weeks later. The caption may be forgotten. The image keeps doing the work.

### 5.3 Generator-specific notes

The image prompt should be tailored to whichever generator we're using. The current preference order:

1. **Nano Banana (Gemini)** - best for editorial-illustration style, character consistency, and Filipino-specific visual references. Default for most posts.
2. **ChatGPT / DALL-E** - useful for cleaner, more diagrammatic images. Better when text-free graphic shapes are needed.
3. **Midjourney** (if available) - painterly, mood-heavy editorial work.

When writing the prompt, include:
- Style (e.g., "editorial cartoon, single panel, line work over flat color")
- Subject (specific, not generic - "an Auckland household opening a dividend cheque, a Manila household opening a Meralco bill, both at kitchen tables")
- Composition (rule of thirds, centered subject, split-screen, etc.)
- Mood / lighting (warm vs cold, daylight vs harsh fluorescent)
- What to AVOID (more on this below)

### 5.4 The text-in-image rule (CRITICAL)

**AI image generators are bad at spelling.** Do not ask any generator to render text inside the image if a misspelling would embarrass the page.

- Numbers (dates, currencies) are especially risky.
- Logos, government seals, and recognizable brands are extra risky (legally too).
- If text is essential, generate the image text-free and add the text as an overlay in a separate step (Canva, Figma, or our publishing workflow).

If text MUST be in the image:
- Use 1-3 words maximum
- Use common English or Tagalog words (no acronyms, no numbers)
- Generate multiple variants and pick the one without spelling errors

### 5.5 The image prompt is part of the research brief

The research brief includes an `Image direction` field with 1-3 sentences. The writer expands this into a full prompt at draft time. The reviewer checks the generated image against the post tone before publishing.

## 6. The Drafting Workflow (writer's POV)

1. **Read the brief** in `/raw/briefs/`. If anything is unclear or thin on sources, push back to research.
2. **Pick the hook formula** (Section 2). Draft 3 hook options. Pick the strongest.
3. **Outline the 5 sections** (Section 1). One bullet each. Don't write prose yet.
4. **Draft the body** in one sitting. Don't optimize. Get to the closer first.
5. **Drop in the Taglish** (Section 4) only where it carries emotional weight.
6. **Write the closer** independently. The closer is its own line, not a continuation. Often the closer is written first or written last; never written automatically.
7. **Cut 20%** on the second pass. TFS posts always benefit from being shorter than the first draft.
8. **Run the sanity check** from `brand-context.md` Section 11. NO EM DASHES. Taglish is complete thoughts. Closer lands. Facts verified. Perspective matches the platform (Section 5).
9. **Save to** `/output/posts/{YYYY-MM-DD}-{slug}-{platform}.md`. One file per platform variant.
10. **Hand off to the image step**, then to the platform-specific reviewer.

## 7. Anti-patterns (what NOT to write)

- **The TED talk:** A post that lectures the reader on civics. We trust the reader.
- **The slogan parade:** Posts that are 80% rallying cries and 20% substance. We earn the slogan with evidence.
- **The doomer:** Posts that leave the reader with no agency. We always close on a verdict the reader can take with them, not despair.
- **The "smart aleck":** Sarcasm without a point. Be sharp, not snide.
- **The translator's Taglish:** Anything that reads like English-to-Tagalog Google Translate dropped in for flavor.
- **The em dash:** It bears repeating. NO EM DASHES.
- **The corporate sign-off:** "What are your thoughts? Comment below!" is for brand pages. We close with a verdict or a question that does its own work.

---

## 8. The "would I screenshot this?" test

Before publishing, scroll the post on your own phone. Find the single line in the post you'd screenshot and send to a friend.

If you can't find one, the post isn't done.

If you find three, the post is the right length.

If every line could be screenshotted, you've over-written. Cut the connectors.

---

## 9. Length guidelines (rough, by platform)

- **Facebook (long-form pillar post):** 400-900 words. Sometimes longer for hybrid news posts.
- **Facebook (reactive / short):** 80-200 words.
- **Reddit (r/Philippines, r/BusinessPH):** 500-1500 words. Reddit rewards depth + sources.
- **Threads:** 300-500 characters per post, threaded for longer narratives.
- **Twitter / X cross-post:** 280 characters per beat, threaded.

Length is downstream of the idea. Don't pad to a target. Don't truncate a strong post to fit a platform - split into a thread instead.

---

## 10. The voice north star

If you finish a draft and can imagine the persona described in `brand-context.md` actually saying it out loud - calmly, at 9 PM, over coffee, to a friend who just asked "why is everything so broken back home?" - you're done.

If it sounds like a campaign ad, an op-ed page, or a LinkedIn manifesto, you're not.
