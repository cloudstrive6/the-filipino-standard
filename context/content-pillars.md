## PILLAR 1 - Governance Comparison (20%)

How basic government works in NZ / developed countries versus how it works in the Philippines. The frame is always: *this isn't impossible. Other countries do it. We're choosing not to.*

### Topics (15)

**1.1 Business registration: NZ vs PH** [PUBLISHED - Reddit AMA]
- **Angle:** Online in under an hour for ~NZD $130 in NZ. In PH it's 13+ steps, SEC, BIR, barangay, mayor's permit, weeks of running around.
- **Image:** Split-screen flowchart, NZ side clean and minimal, PH side a tangled spaghetti diagram.

**1.2 Electricity ownership: Entrust dividend vs Meralco bill** [PUBLISHED - FB long-form]
- **Angle:** Entrust paid PHP 13,000 per Auckland household in 2025. Meralco has never paid you back, ever.
- **Image:** Editorial cartoon, NZ household opening a dividend cheque, PH household opening a bill with the same number.

**1.3 Healthcare: NZ public system vs PhilHealth reality**
- **Angle:** "Free at the point of care" vs "PhilHealth covers a fraction, you still pay everything." Not romanticizing NZ wait times, but the floor is different.
- **Image:** Two hospital reception desks, side by side, signage tells the story.

**1.4 Public transport: Auckland Transport / Wellington Metro vs MRT/LRT/EDSA**
- **Angle:** Integrated payment cards, real schedules, accountability when delayed. Vs MRT breakdowns being a national event.
- **Image:** A clean train timetable vs a packed EDSA bus on its side.

**1.5 Disaster response: Christchurch earthquake vs Yolanda / annual typhoons**
- **Angle:** Government's prime duty is to serve and protect. How rebuilding actually happens vs how aid disappears.
- **Image:** Before-and-after rebuild photos, time-lapse style.

**1.6 Tax systems: IRD (NZ) vs BIR (PH)**
- **Angle:** myIR is an online portal. You file in minutes. BIR is queues, missing forms, "come back tomorrow." Both governments tax you, only one respects your time.
- **Image:** A laptop with myIR open vs a long queue snaking out of a BIR office.

**1.7 Public schools: NZ decile system vs PH multi-shift overcrowded classrooms**
- **Angle:** Funding follows the child, schools are accountable. Vs 60 kids per room in 2 shifts.
- **Image:** Two classrooms, same age kids, different reality.

**1.8 Police accountability: IPCA (NZ) vs Internal Affairs in PH**
- **Angle:** Independent oversight that actually has teeth vs investigations that mysteriously close.
- **Image:** Two badges, one with a magnifying glass over it, one in a drawer.

**1.9 Water utilities: Watercare (NZ) vs Maynilad / Manila Water concessionaires**
- **Angle:** Public utility vs private concessions with rate hikes built into the contract.
- **Image:** A glass of tap water in Auckland vs a row of stored water containers in a Manila house.

**1.10 Building permits: NZ council process vs PH 11+ signatures**
- **Angle:** Single application, set timeline, fixed fees vs an obstacle course where every signature is a fee.
- **Image:** A clean digital form vs a stack of stamped forms.

**1.11 Social welfare: Working for Families (NZ) vs 4Ps**
- **Angle:** Conditional cash transfer programs exist in both. The difference is structural: NZ supports the working poor too, not just the very poor.
- **Image:** Family at a kitchen table, two budgets side by side.

**1.12 Road maintenance: NZTA-managed state highways vs DPWH potholes**
- **Angle:** Maintenance schedules, audited contractors, lifespan of asphalt. Vs the annual ritual of "ginagawa pa lang noon, sira na ulit."
- **Image:** Two roads, one freshly maintained, one with a permanent pothole.

**1.13 Internet pricing: NZ fibre standard vs PGN-Converge-Globe duopoly**
- **Angle:** NZ has ~30 ISPs competing on a regulated fibre wholesale layer. PH has effectively 3 players, and the price reflects it.
- **Image:** A speed-test screenshot pair, price-per-Mbps headline.

**1.14 Government websites: govt.nz vs PH agency websites that haven't been updated since 2014**
- **Angle:** A government website is a statement of how much the state respects citizens' time.
- **Image:** Two homepage screenshots, the contrast does the work.

**1.15 Minimum wage vs cost of living**
- **Angle:** NZ minimum wage relative to a basket of basic goods vs PH regional minimum wages relative to the same basket.
- **Image:** Two grocery receipts, same items, different shock value.

**2.7 Politician luxury vs public poverty**

**2.10 Political revenge vs public service**

**2.14 Unfinished government projects**

**2.15 Utang na loob in politics**

- **Image:** A handshake with a price tag dangling from it.

## PILLAR 3 - Constitutional Awareness (10%)

Most Filipinos have never been walked through what the Constitution actually says. We treat it as a living document, not a museum piece.

### Topics (10)

**3.1 What "sovereignty resides in the people" actually means**
- **Angle:** Article II, Section 1, verbatim. Then: in plain English, this is the line politicians fear most.
- **Image:** The Constitution open to that exact line, highlighted.

**3.2 The right to petition the government**
- **Angle:** Article III, Section 4. Petitioning isn't an act of disloyalty, it's a right.
- **Image:** A protest sign rewritten as a constitutional quote.

**3.3 Freedom of speech in practice vs on paper**
- **Angle:** What the text says. What journalists, citizens, and critics actually experience. The gap is the story.
- **Image:** A microphone with a constitutional citation taped to it.

**3.4 The government's "prime duty" - and what it implies**
- **Angle:** Article II, Section 4. "Serve and protect" is the floor, not the ceiling. Disaster response, infrastructure, public health.
- **Image:** A government building with the article carved above the door.

**3.5 What the Constitution actually says about public utilities**
- **Angle:** Foreign ownership caps, franchise requirements, the public interest standard. Used to defend monopolies that don't serve the public.
- **Image:** A power line with a constitutional clause as the wire.

**3.6 FOIA in PH: Executive Order vs a real law**
- **Angle:** We have an executive order on freedom of information for the executive branch. We do not have a Freedom of Information Act. That gap matters.
- **Image:** A filing cabinet with one drawer open, the rest padlocked.

**3.7 Transparency provisions of the Constitution**
- **Angle:** Article II, Section 28. "Full public disclosure of all its transactions involving public interest." Cite, then ask: when did you last see this honored?
- **Image:** A glass wall with documents behind it.

**3.8 Right to assembly**
- **Angle:** Article III, Section 4 again. Peaceful assembly is constitutionally protected. Permit regimes that effectively block protests are constitutionally questionable.
- **Image:** An empty plaza with the article overlaid.

**3.9 What "public trust" actually means**
- **Angle:** Public office is a public trust. Article XI, Section 1. Trust is a fiduciary standard, not a vibe.
- **Image:** A scale with "public office" on one side and "trust" on the other.

**3.10 The anti-dynasty provision Congress has refused to define for 38 years**
- **Angle:** Article II, Section 26 forbids political dynasties "as may be defined by law." Congress has never passed the enabling law. That's not a bug, that's the feature.
- **Image:** An hourglass with the date 1987 at the top, still running.

## PILLAR 4 - Economic & Utility Reform (15%)

The structural economics of why Filipinos pay more for less. This pillar is where receipts matter most.

### Topics (15)

**4.1 Meralco ownership: never community-owned, never will be** [PUBLISHED - FB]
- **Angle:** 1903 American-owned, 1962 Lopez, 1972 Marcos cronies, 1986 Lopez again, 2008-present MPIC + JG Summit. Always private. Always extractive.
- **Image:** A timeline of logos behind a single power pylon.

**4.2 Why PH electricity is among the most expensive in Asia**
- **Angle:** Generation mix, transmission costs, taxes on power, regulatory capture. Cost is not destiny.
- **Image:** A regional bar chart of electricity prices, PH bar in a different color.

**4.3 Water concessionaires: Maynilad, Manila Water, and the contract that won't die**
- **Angle:** The 1997 privatization, the arbitration awards, who actually pays for "inefficiency."
- **Image:** A water tap dripping over a contract.

**4.4 Toll road profits in PH**
- **Angle:** SLEX, NLEX, Skyway. Who owns the concessions, what the IRR looks like, what the public gets back.
- **Image:** A toll gate with a calculator above it.

**4.5 Internet duopoly: PLDT and Globe (and the polite fiction of competition)** [PRIORITY]
- **Angle:** Two players, similar pricing, similar speeds. A third entrant promised every cycle, never materially shifts the market.
- **Image:** A speed-test app with the same number on two phones.

**4.6 Rigged bidding: how government contracts repeatedly land with the same names**
- **Angle:** Pre-qualification rules, "lowest calculated responsive bid," and the magic of repeat winners.
- **Image:** A bidding podium with three identical envelopes.

**4.7 13+ steps to start a business in PH**
- **Angle:** SEC / DTI, BIR, mayor's permit, barangay clearance, sanitary permit, fire safety, etc. Compared to NZ's one form.
- **Image:** A long checklist scroll unrolling off a table.

**4.8 BIR nightmare: the small business compliance burden**
- **Angle:** Books of account, OR/SI registration, withholding tax slips, quarterly filings, annual ITR. Designed for compliance, not growth.
- **Image:** A small business owner buried in pink and blue receipts.

**4.9 OFW remittances: the country's largest "industry" is its diaspora**
- **Angle:** ~$38B annually. The economy is propped up by Filipinos who had to leave. That's a policy failure, not a success story.
- **Image:** A map of the world with money flowing back to one country.

**4.10 Housing affordability: why even middle-class Filipinos can't buy**
- **Angle:** Price-to-income ratios, interest rates, the lack of a real public housing program.
- **Image:** A condo skyline with "For Sale" signs that never come down.

**4.11 Fuel prices: taxes, the peso, and the global oil cycle**
- **Angle:** Why PH pump prices move the way they do. Excise tax under TRAIN. Who actually absorbs the cost.
- **Image:** A gas pump with a tax pie chart on the side.

**4.12 Rice importation: why a rice-eating country imports rice**
- **Angle:** The Rice Tariffication Law, smuggling, NFA's neutered role. A food security question that rarely gets honest answers.
- **Image:** A rice grain with a passport stamp.

**4.13 PhilHealth: where the contributions actually go**
- **Angle:** Coverage gaps, fraud cases, the COA audit findings. Every working Filipino pays in. What comes out is uneven.
- **Image:** A piggy bank with a hospital invoice taped to it.

**4.14 SSS and Pag-IBIG: contribution-to-benefit math**
- **Angle:** How much you put in over 30 years vs what you actually receive. Compare to KiwiSaver / equivalents.
- **Image:** Two columns, contributions vs benefits, dramatically uneven.

**4.15 Banking: why a stable employee can't get a loan, but a politician's nephew can**
- **Angle:** Collateral-based lending, weak credit scoring, the role of relationship banking.
- **Image:** A bank vault door with a "Members Only" sign.

## PILLAR 5 - Filipino Empowerment (15%)

This is the heart of the page. The frame is never "Filipinos are bad" or "the Philippines is hopeless." It is always: *you have been short-changed and the system has spent decades teaching you to be grateful for it.*

### Topics (15)

**5.1 The "short-changed" realization** [PUBLISHED - FB]
- **Angle:** The moment you realize what you've been paying for is not what other countries pay for.
- **Image:** A receipt with the wrong total, circled in red.

**5.2 Stop normalizing suffering**
- **Angle:** Smiling through the typhoon is not resilience. It's a coping mechanism the powerful have monetized.
- **Image:** A smiling face emoji with the smile drawn on top of a frown.

**5.3 "Ganyan talaga" is not an analysis** [PRIORITY]
- **Angle:** The phrase that ends every conversation about reform. It is not a fact, it is a surrender.
- **Image:** The phrase written in chalk, slowly erased.

**5.4 You deserve first-world public services**
- **Angle:** Not because you migrated, not because you earned it abroad. Because you are a citizen of a republic and that is what citizenship is supposed to mean.
- **Image:** A passport laid open, the constitution as the visa stamp.

**5.5 Patriotism vs blind loyalty**
- **Angle:** Real patriotism is demanding more, not defending less. The "love it or leave it" frame is a control mechanism.
- **Image:** A flag held by two hands, one open, one fist.

**5.6 Demanding better is not ungrateful**
- **Angle:** Especially aimed at OFWs and the diaspora. You sent money home. You bought your parents a house. You are not ungrateful. You are paying attention.
- **Image:** A bayanihan illustration where the house being lifted is "accountability."

**5.7 Raising the standard (the tagline post)**
- **Angle:** What it means to "raise the standard." Not perfection. Just the floor.
- **Image:** A standard being literally raised, dawn behind it.

**5.8 The power of an informed electorate**
- **Angle:** Vote-buying only works because information is scarce and parties are weak. Both can change.
- **Image:** A voter holding a ballot with a magnifying glass.

**5.9 The diaspora perspective shift** [PUBLISHED - FB / Reddit]
- **Angle:** What you can only see clearly from the outside. NZ, Canada, Singapore. The contrast is the diagnosis.
- **Image:** A Filipino looking back at PH from a foreign airport window.

**5.10 Stop celebrating bare minimum**
- **Angle:** Cleaning the road for a state visit is not progress. It is theater.
- **Image:** A photo op being staged in front of a freshly painted wall.

**5.11 A vote is a hiring decision**
- **Angle:** Imagine hiring an employee the way we elect officials. Name recognition only. No interview. No reference check.
- **Image:** A job interview where the candidate just holds up a tarpaulin.

**5.12 Filipino workers thrive abroad. The problem isn't the worker.**
- **Angle:** Nurses, engineers, IT, hospitality. The diaspora's success is the country's indictment.
- **Image:** A Filipino name tag on a foreign uniform.

**5.13 The "diskarte" trap**
- **Angle:** Diskarte is admirable as a survival skill. As a national identity, it lets the state off the hook. Citizens shouldn't have to outsmart their own government to access basic services.
- **Image:** A maze where the exit is also a maze.

**5.14 Brain drain isn't the cause. It's the symptom.**
- **Angle:** People don't leave because they don't love the country. They leave because the country has not been built to keep them.
- **Image:** A row of NAIA departure boards, all destinations.

**5.15 The right to be angry**
- **Angle:** Anger, channeled, is a civic act. The "be more positive" reflex is how dysfunctional systems protect themselves.
- **Image:** A controlled flame, contained but not extinguished.

## PILLAR 6 - Business & SME Advocacy (20%)

The Filipino entrepreneur's case. Written from the persona's lived experience: ran an F&B business in the Philippines, now co-runs a B2B tech / AI / automation company in NZ. This pillar speaks directly to the millions of Filipinos trying to build something legitimate and the system that makes it harder than it has to be. Attack the friction. Defend the builder.

### Topics (20)

**6.1 The 13+ step business registration gauntlet (SME edition)** [PRIORITY]
- **Angle:** Drilled deeper than Pillar 4.7. Walk the reader, step by step, through SEC / DTI, BIR, mayor's permit, barangay, sanitary, fire safety, and the lesser-known stops. Each step a fee. Each fee a small kingdom. Compare to one-form NZ.
- **Image:** A literal map of Metro Manila with pins at every required office, lines connecting them like a treasure hunt.

**6.2 BIR for the small business owner: a survival map**
- **Angle:** Books of account. OR / SI registration. Withholding tax. Quarterly filings. Annual ITR. A working guide written like a friend who's done it twice.
- **Image:** A small business desk with the BIR form 1701Q half-visible, coffee, calculator, exhaustion.

**6.3 Why most Filipino businesses stay "informal" - and what it costs them**
- **Angle:** ~70%+ of PH employment is informal. Informal isn't laziness, it's a rational response to the cost of being formal. The cost is real: no banking access, no e-commerce platform onboarding, no growth.
- **Image:** A sari-sari store with a "Closed for permit renewal" sign and a kid waiting outside.

**6.4 The local government permit gauntlet**
- **Angle:** Mayor's permit, barangay clearance, sanitary permit, fire safety inspection, sometimes locational clearance, sometimes electrical inspection. Every signature has a fee, an unofficial fee, or both.
- **Image:** A stack of permits with different colored stamps, the same business name typed on each.

**6.5 Banking for SMEs: why a salaried employee gets a car loan and your business doesn't**
- **Angle:** PH banking lends against payslips, not against business cash flow. The result: a 10-year track record of healthy receipts means less than 3 months of employee payslips.
- **Image:** A bank teller window with two queues, one labeled "Employed," moving fast.

**6.6 Credit scoring, or the absence of it, in PH lending**
- **Angle:** CIC (Credit Information Corporation) exists. Most banks still don't really use it. SME lending in PH is relationship-based and that locks out anyone without connections.
- **Image:** A credit report on one side, a handshake on the other, with the handshake winning.

**6.7 SME payment infrastructure: GCash, Maya, bank transfer, and the missing pieces**
- **Angle:** Payment rails have come a long way (InstaPay, PESONet). What's still missing: clean payout reconciliation, recurring billing for B2B, true business accounts. NZ comparison: a bank-issued business EFTPOS terminal in 3 days.
- **Image:** A phone screen with three payment apps open, all reading slightly different amounts.

**6.8 E-commerce for Filipino SMEs: platform dependency on Shopee and Lazada**
- **Angle:** The platforms moved Filipino SMEs online faster than any government program ever did. They also extract: take rates, ad spend, algorithm dependency. Building outside the platforms is hard. Inside them you don't own the customer.
- **Image:** A Shopee box with a small store name on the return address, lost in a pile of identical boxes.

**6.9 DTI Negosyo Centers and PH "SME support": brochure vs reality**
- **Angle:** On paper, the support exists. In practice, the Negosyo Center is often understaffed, under-resourced, and not where SME owners actually go for help. Compare to NZ business.govt.nz and Callaghan Innovation.
- **Image:** A government office with a single chair in front of an empty desk and a poster behind it.

**6.10 The OFW-returnee entrepreneur: capital, no roadmap**
- **Angle:** Years of remittances become a lump sum. The lump sum becomes a sari-sari, a jeep, a tricycle. Most fail within 2 years. Not because of effort. Because nobody trained the system to receive returning capital well.
- **Image:** A balikbayan box opened, full of small business signage instead of clothes.

**6.11 Why the franchise model dominates PH small business**
- **Angle:** Franchise looks like the safe path. It often is, in the short term. The ceiling is real: most franchisees never out-earn an upper-middle salary, while the franchisor banks the brand value.
- **Image:** A row of identical milk-tea storefronts, one franchisee inside each.

**6.12 Hiring as an SME: skills gap, salary squeeze, retention**
- **Angle:** PH has talent. PH education doesn't always produce the specific skills SMEs need. SMEs then can't match BPO / multinational pay. Retention becomes survival.
- **Image:** A job posting on a bulletin board, four sticky notes on it, all asking different salary ranges.

**6.13 Labor laws on a 5-person team**
- **Angle:** PH labor code was written for big employers. SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, 13th month, holiday pay, regularization rules. The intent is worker protection. The unintended effect: SMEs avoid hiring formally at all.
- **Image:** A small office where the boss and the bookkeeper are the same person, surrounded by forms.

**6.14 Tech adoption by Filipino SMEs: where the leverage actually is**
- **Angle:** Most Filipino SMEs are still using paper logbooks, manual invoicing, no CRM. The cheapest, fastest wins are not "digital transformation." They're a shared spreadsheet, an inventory app, a payment link.
- **Image:** A laptop next to a paper logbook, an arrow drawn from one to the other.

**6.15 AI and automation for Filipino SMEs (the persona's lane)** [PRIORITY]
- **Angle:** AI for Filipino SMEs is not ChatGPT-or-bust. It's: messenger auto-replies, inventory forecasting, bookkeeping co-pilots, basic web automation. The persona's company exists in this space. Speak with authority but without selling.
- **Image:** A small business owner at her counter, phone in hand, calmly approving an AI-drafted reply.

**6.16 Family business succession: the second-generation problem**
- **Angle:** The first generation built it. The second generation went to school for something else. Succession in PH SMEs is often emotional, not structural. Globally, only ~30% of family businesses survive into G2; in PH the rate is harsher.
- **Image:** A founder's photo on the wall, the new desk underneath empty.

**6.17 The side hustle economy**
- **Angle:** When a primary salary doesn't cover rent and food, employees become reluctant entrepreneurs. The side hustle is the most honest unemployment number in the country. It's also where future SMEs are born.
- **Image:** A laptop screen split between an office email and a meal-prep order list.

**6.18 Government procurement: how SMEs are locked out of public contracts**
- **Angle:** PhilGEPS exists. The bid requirements (track record, bonds, accreditation, cash flow) effectively pre-qualify only large, well-connected players. SMEs that could deliver are filtered out before evaluation.
- **Image:** A PhilGEPS bid page with a "Submit" button that's greyed out.

**6.19 The cost of being compliant vs the cost of being caught**
- **Angle:** The math for many SMEs: full compliance can eat 15-25% of margin. Being caught informal costs less than that, sometimes. The system has accidentally taught entrepreneurs that informality is the rational choice. That's a design failure.
- **Image:** A scale with "Compliance fees" on one side, "Risk of being caught" on the other, the scale tipped.

**6.20 Cross-border / export from PH: what it actually takes to sell abroad**
- **Angle:** Customs, FDA, BOC, banking for foreign currency receipts, Stripe / Wise / PayPal limitations on PH-registered entities. Filipino-made products compete globally. Filipino-registered businesses often can't.
- **Image:** A pallet of locally made goods at the port, a forklift driver checking a 12-page form.

## How to use this file

When planning the calendar:

1. Pull topics in roughly the mix above (20/20/10/15/15/20).
2. Never schedule two posts from the same pillar back-to-back unless one is reactive (breaking news).
3. Update [PUBLISHED] tags as posts go live, and add the publish date next to the tag.
4. When a topic is published, **don't retire it forever.** Plan a 6-month callback post that builds on the original, references it, and adds new evidence. Callbacks are how we build a connected narrative instead of disposable posts.
