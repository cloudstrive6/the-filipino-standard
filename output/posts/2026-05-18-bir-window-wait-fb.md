## Caption

A small business owner can spend an entire morning at a BIR window just to file one quarterly form.

Walang exception. Doesn't matter if you have a CPA. Doesn't matter if the form is short. The queue is the queue, and the queue is hours long.

Yes, the eFPS portal exists. Yes, online filing is technically a thing. But ask any MSME owner who has tried to use it for a corrected return, a switched-account adjustment, or anything beyond the simplest case. They will tell you the same story: the system flags it, the system asks for a physical document, the system sends them back to the window.

In New Zealand, the same MSME owner files their GST return online in under fifteen minutes. The IRD portal handles corrections, adjustments, and amended returns without sending anyone anywhere. It's not perfect, no country is, but it doesn't bleed productive hours from people whose entire job is running a small business.

That's the real cost. Not the tax itself. The compliance theatre around it. The half-day spent in a queue is half a day not spent serving customers, training staff, or building anything.

Filipino MSMEs are told they're the backbone of the economy. Then the backbone is asked to spend its mornings in line.

Tama na.
