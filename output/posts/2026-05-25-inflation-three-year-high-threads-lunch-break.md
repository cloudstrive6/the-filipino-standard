Inflation just hit a three-year high. Diesel up 122 percent. Gasoline up 59 percent. Even rice and cereals, in a rice-eating country, up 11 percent.

Tama na ang pagtitiis.

The shock came from abroad. The exposure was built at home. Other countries cushion their people: fuel reserves, targeted subsidies, transport that absorbs the blow. Here it lands straight on the dinner table.

A global crisis exposed a local choice.
