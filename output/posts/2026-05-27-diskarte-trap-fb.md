Filipinos are praised for their diskarte like it is a national superpower.

And in a way, it is.

But every act of diskarte is also a quiet receipt for something the system was supposed to do, and didn't.

Think about what diskarte actually looks like day to day. Knowing someone who can make the permit move faster. Finding the one window that is actually open. Improvising a whole new route home when the train stops for the third time this month. Picking up a side hustle because one salary no longer covers rent and food. Filipinos are world-class at this. The skill is real, and it is genuinely admirable.

But step back and ask what all that brilliance is actually for.

Diskarte is the human patch over an institutional hole. The faster the permit should have been, the more diskarte it takes to survive the wait. The more often the service fails, the more ingenuity ordinary people have to spend just to get what they already paid for. The skill is not the achievement. The skill is the cost.

And here is the trap. When a country starts to treat diskarte as its identity, it stops asking why diskarte is needed at all. The workaround becomes the culture. The culture becomes the excuse. And the institution that failed in the first place quietly gets to keep failing, because the people are so good at absorbing the damage that nobody upstream ever feels it.

So the public pays twice. Once in taxes, for the service. Again in energy, outsmarting the very thing the taxes were supposed to buy.

In a functioning system, the default citizen does not need a workaround. No country runs perfectly, but the floor is supposed to be simple: basic services work without a contact, a fixer, or a favor.

Diskarte should be the spice, not the staple. It is what you reach for when life throws something rare and unexpected at you. It was never meant to be the daily tax on being Filipino.

Filipinos are not short on talent. They are short on a system worthy of it.

Bakit kailangan pang mag-diskarte para sa bagay na dapat ay gumagana na?

When was the last time you had to "diskarte" your way through something the government should have just handled?
