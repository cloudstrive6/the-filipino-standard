The loudest patriotism is often the laziest.

"Love it or leave it." "Don't complain, be grateful." "At least we're resilient."

Real patriotism asks more of a country, not less.

There is a script handed to almost anyone who criticizes how things are run here. You are being negative. You are ungrateful. You are making the country look bad. If you hate it so much, why not just leave. It sounds like love of country. It works like a muzzle.

Here is the quiet trick inside that script.

Blind loyalty survives by blurring two very different things into one: the country, and the people currently in charge of it. Once those two are treated as the same, any criticism of the management starts to feel like an attack on the nation itself. So people stay silent, and call the silence patriotism.

But the country and its current managers are not the same thing. The Philippines is its people, its land, its language, its history. The people running it at any given moment are temporary. You can love the first with your whole chest and still refuse to defend what is being done to it by the second.

Think about the OFWs who send money home every month and still ask hard questions about where the country is going. They bought their parents a house. They funded a sibling's schooling. Calling them ungrateful is not just wrong, it is backwards. They are not ungrateful. They are paying attention.

Demanding better is the higher form of love, not the betrayal of it. It is the parent who expects more because they believe in the child. The opposite of love was never criticism. The opposite of love is indifference. The person who has stopped demanding anything from this country is the one who has actually stopped hoping for it.

No country is perfect, and the healthiest ones argue loudly about their own flaws precisely because they expect better of themselves. Silence is not a sign of love. It is a sign that someone has given up, or been told to.

A Filipino can love this country enough to refuse to lie about it. That is not the weakest version of patriotism. It is the strongest one available.

Hindi pagmamahal ang bulag na pagsunod.

When was the last time someone told you that criticizing the country meant you did not love it?
