Every time you tap through a toll plaza, you are paying a private company for the right to drive.

The road serves a public need. The profit flows to a private few.

And the contracts are written so the toll mostly moves in one direction: up.

The expressways that keep Luzon moving are not run the way most people assume. NLEX is operated by Metro Pacific's tollways group. SLEX and the Metro Manila Skyway system are operated by San Miguel Corporation. These are not short arrangements. They are concessions that run for decades. The NLEX-SLEX connector alone was signed as a 37-year contract.

So the roads everyone depends on to commute and to move goods are, in practice, long-term private assets.

Here is how the arrangement actually works. The public provides the demand. Everyone needs to get to work, and almost everything in the stores travelled down one of these expressways to get there. The concessionaire collects the toll. And periodic toll increases are typically built right into the agreements, adjusted on a schedule rather than earned through visibly better service.

That is the part worth sitting with. A toll that rises on a timetable, no matter what is happening to the average commuter's wages, is not really a market price. It is a tollbooth on a necessity.

There is nothing inherently wrong with private companies building or operating roads. They can do it well. The question The Filipino Standard keeps coming back to is a different one. When essential infrastructure becomes a guaranteed-return private asset, who is the deal actually designed to serve, the commuter or the concession?

And it is the same shape the page keeps finding everywhere. A handful of dominant names. Prices that ratchet upward by design. A public with no real way to opt out, because there is no other road.

Some countries keep their core highways public, or cap the returns a private operator can take from a captive public. No country is perfect, but those are choices, and they are choices the Philippines is allowed to make too.

A road everyone is forced to use should not be a guaranteed profit for a chosen few.
