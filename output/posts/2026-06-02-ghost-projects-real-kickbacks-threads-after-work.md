## Why this works

- Hook is a hard number ("P573 million") tied to an impossible image ("projects that were never built"). The reader cannot scroll past the contradiction. Curiosity stop in two seconds.
- One strong idea: the only thing fake about the flood control scandal was the infrastructure. Everything else, including the consequences for citizens, is real. Different angle from the morning post (which framed the same news as "a chamber on strike").
- The chiasmus reframe "The projects were ghosts. The kickbacks were not." is the screenshot. Two short clauses, perfect parallelism, devastating verdict.
- List-of-three rhythm in the middle ("One senator...", "Sixteen contractors...", "Up to sixty more...") tracks the widening accountability net without naming a single individual.
- The third beat reframes who actually delivered accountability: "Years of marches" (citizens) and "The Sandiganbayan" (the judiciary) acted. The Senate majority is the only body named that did not.
- Closer is a pre-cleared safe Tagalog verdict ("Tama na.") at the planner-mandated `closing_line` slot. Not in the recent-5 phrases-to-avoid window. Complete thought, not sprinkled.
- No individual politician or administration named. Argument is structural (one branch, one chamber, the contractor class). Stays re-shareable across political tribes.
- Zero em dashes. Zero first-person pronouns. Third-person throughout.
- 333 characters: well under the 500 ceiling, breathing room between beats for mobile scroll.
