## Why this works

- Hook opens with a pre-cleared Tagalog verdict (`Ganun kalala.`) followed immediately by a concrete news anchor (arrest, Monday). Scroll stops twice in three lines.
- Single strong idea: the Senate majority walked out because a colleague was arrested. Everything else in the post is evidence for that one claim.
- List-of-three format that PH audiences read rhythmically. Each beat is a real, named stalled bill, not a generic abstraction.
- The 250,000 BHW figure is the specificity that converts abstraction into damage. The reader can count the human cost without being told to.
- Closer is a four-word reframe: "A chamber on strike." Reframes "Senate independence" as labor action by the people paid to legislate. Screenshot-worthy.
- No individual politician named. Argument is structural (the chamber, not the senator). Stays re-shareable across political tribes.
- Zero em dashes. Zero first-person pronouns. Third-person on Threads as required.
- 351 characters: well under 500, breathing room, mobile-scroll-friendly.
